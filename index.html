Legacy Network Vulnerabilities

Prior to the re-engineering model, MTC suffered from major structural network constraints that accelerated costs:

Capital Trapped in Inventory

Maintained 4.5 months of inventory buffers across networks, significantly lagging behind the 2.5-month lean industry standard.

Logistical Redundancy

A multi-tiered, fragmented routing path (Manufacturer → 3PL A → Mid-mile → 3PL B → Hospital) caused heavy double-handling and lack of pipeline tracking.

Commercial Force Misalignment

Highly trained field sales teams spent roughly 40% of their operational hours moving boxes, tracking manual items, and managing regional supply tasks.

Manufacturing Whiplash

Plant production levels were completely exposed to immediate market demand spikes, swinging wildly from 49k down to 1.8k units and inflating unit runs.

The 5-Pillar Redesign Network

1. Direct-to-3PL Logistical Pipeline

Bypassed intermediate distribution nodes to ship bulk freight straight to a single centralized 3PL Sterilization Hub. Slashed order cycle durations from 14 down to 6 days.

2. Hub-and-Spoke Pooling Mechanics

Aggregated protective safety levels at larger regional distribution hubs, compressing immediate on-site pipeline footprints cleanly to the 2.5-month lean benchmark.

3. Commercial Force Decoupling

Automated routing flows directly from 3PL lines to hospitals, re-allocating sales rep activities from back-end administrative logistics to 80% specialized clinical consultant focus.

4. Cloud-Enabled RFID Kiosks

Deployed real-time automated RFID inventory spaces right inside hospital stock areas. Fe feeds live product consumption visibility directly into production tracking loops.

5. Chase-with-Buffer Production Logic

Decoupled daily factory lines from direct market anomalies by running a smoothed baseline production output (32.5k units) supported by safety layers.
